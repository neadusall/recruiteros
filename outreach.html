Sequence methodology

Multi-step, multi-channel, all personalized

Each step is drafted from the prospect's real history and the triggering signal, never a mail-merge. Mix channels and waits to match how real relationships build.

  • Email → LinkedIn → SMS → voice in any order, with waits
  • Voice notes, auto-generate a personal audio touch above a set interest threshold
  • Smart waits that pause when a reply lands
  • Breakup logic that closes the loop gracefully

The rapport-first method

Earn the reply before you ask for anything

People say yes to people they trust, and trust is built in the touches before the pitch. Every RecruiterOS sequence is engineered to build rapport first, so by the time the offer lands it feels like a favor, not a cold ask.

1Recognize · no ask

Lead with genuine, specific recognition

Open with something only a real person who studied their work could say. No pitch, no link, nothing to react to except a compliment they have earned.

"Anja, I went down a rabbit hole on the Trade Republic order-entry flow this week. The way it handles partial fills without a full re-render is genuinely clever. Just wanted to say it's some of the sharpest trading UI I've come across."Touch 1 · LinkedIn or email · 0 asks
2Relate · no ask

Offer an insight tied to a real signal

Show you understand their world right now. Anchor on a signal, a reorg, a launch, a funding round, and share a useful observation. Still no ask.

"Saw the team just reorganized the web platform group. Usually when that happens, the people who quietly held the architecture together get handed more surface area, not more ownership. Curious whether that's how it's playing out for you."Touch 2 · 2 days later · still no ask
3Invite · soft

Ask permission, not for their time

A tiny, easy yes. You are not asking for a call yet, only for permission to share. The opt-out makes it feel respectful, which makes people say yes.

"I work with a few teams building greenfield React platforms, and one feels weirdly aligned with how you think about UI. Open to me sending the one-paragraph version? Totally fine if not."Touch 3 · 2 days later · micro-yes
4Pitch · the ask

Now make it concrete and easy to accept

Only after rapport is built do you pitch, and you make it specific, credible, and trivially easy to say yes to. Real numbers, real details, one clear next step.

"Here's the one-paragraph version: fully remote, React and TypeScript, you own the architecture from day one, $120k to $145k. The founder shipped two design systems you'd recognize on sight. Worth 15 minutes Thursday afternoon?"Touch 4 · after the yes · the offer
5Release · graceful

Close the loop with value, not pressure

If the timing is wrong, leave them better than you found them. A graceful exit that gives value keeps the relationship alive for the next role.

"I'll stop here so I'm not that recruiter. If the timing is ever right, my door is open. Either way, sending a short write-up on how the team versions design tokens, thought you'd genuinely enjoy it."Touch 5 · breakup · door left open
