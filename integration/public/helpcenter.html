RecruitersOS Help Center

RecruitersOS brings sourcing, multi-channel outreach, automated screening, every reply, and your pipeline into one command center, so your team works from a single screen instead of juggling tools. This guide walks through every screen and how to set the platform up to go live.

Overview

Everything you do lives in the Command Center: find people, reach out across channels, let automation handle the first pass, work your replies, and move deals through your pipeline. The platform runs two work modes and you pick which one you're in at any moment:

👤

Recruiting

Source and place candidates: build a pipeline, screen, and book interviews.

🏢

Business Development

Win and grow clients: find companies hiring now and book discovery calls.

Each mode has its own campaigns, audiences, and dashboards. Use the BD / Recruiting toggle in the sidebar to switch; the whole workspace re-scopes to that mode.

What's new

  • Make it yours. Upload your logo (a separate one for dark and light appearance), set your brand name and accent color, and run the portal on your own web address.
  • AI Vetting. Automated phone screening in your own cloned voice, every call recorded, transcribed, and scored.
  • Setup hub. One place to connect your tools and track launch readiness at a glance.
  • Campaign Sequences Library. Reusable multi-channel templates you can drop into any campaign.
  • Hire Signals. Companies hiring right now, surfaced as fresh BD leads.

Quick start

  1. Sign in and set your appearance (light or dark) from the account menu in the top-right.
  2. Open Setup and work the launch checklist: connect your tools, set up email sending, connect your ATS, and set up your phone tools for voice and texting.
  3. Make it yours in Setup → Branding (logo, name, accent color), and optionally add your own domain in Setup → Custom domain.
  4. Invite your recruiters from Team. They get their own Recruiter Portal.
  5. Build a campaign, add an audience, and launch. Replies land in your inbox; booked meetings and pipeline show on the Dashboard.
Required tools must show green in Setup before a mode's campaigns can activate, that's your "ready to launch" gate.

Your team & roles

Add recruiters from the Team tab. They receive an invite, sign in, and land in their Recruiter Portal. Admins run the workspace (setup, branding, billing, and integrations) while recruiters do the daily work and are walled off from anything that touches credentials or billing. You can change a person's role at any time.

The Setup hub

One place to stand everything up before you launch. A launch-readiness checklist shows what's done and what still needs attention. Setup is organized into sections:

  • Integrations: connect the tools your workspace uses.
  • Email sending: your sending domains and mailboxes.
  • ATS: your system of record.
  • Voice Drops & AI Vetting: your phone tools and cloned voice.
  • Branding: your logo, name, and color.
  • Custom domain: run the portal on your own web address.

Connecting your tools

Each tool connects right here in the portal, no waiting on anyone. Open a tool, follow its short steps, paste your key, Save, then Test. Status walks from red (not set up) to yellow (saved) to green (verified). All required tools must be green before that mode's campaigns can activate.

The tools you bring, by category:

  • LinkedIn automation: send connection requests, messages, and voice notes from your seat.
  • Contact enrichment: fill in titles, companies, emails, and phone numbers.
  • Job-posting signals: power Hire Signals and "the role they're hiring for."
  • Texting and your phone service: for OS Text, Voice Drops, and AI Vetting.
Your accounts stay yours. Every workspace connects its own tools, kept private and separate from everyone else's. Where your provider offers it, your provider may instead enable a tool for you on a billed basis.

Email sending

Set up your sending domains and mailboxes so outreach email goes out reliably. Add a domain and it is provisioned for you with the right records, warm-up, and rotation built in, so your deliverability stays healthy as you scale.

Your ATS

Connect your applicant tracking system as your single source of truth. People and companies sync into the platform, and every touch the platform makes is written back, so your ATS always reflects reality. Connect it once in Setup and keep working where you already do.

Phone & texting

Voice (Voice Drops, AI Vetting, the dialer) and SMS (OS Text and AI texting) run on your own Telnyx account. You bring your Telnyx API key and a phone number; the platform does the calling and texting through it. Before US carriers will deliver your business texts you must complete a one-time 10DLC registration (also called A2P) in Telnyx. Voice calls don't need 10DLC — but you set the number up in the same place, so do both at once.

What 10DLC is (and why it's required)

10DLC = Application-to-Person messaging over a 10-digit long code. US carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) only deliver business SMS from numbers tied to a registered Brand (your company) and Campaign (your use case). Until that's approved, texts are filtered or blocked. You register once; then every number on that campaign sends reliably.

Set it up in Telnyx — step by step

  1. Create your Telnyx account at telnyx.com and add a payment method (Billing → Payments). 10DLC registration and numbers require billing to be active.
  2. Create an API key: Account → API Keys → Create API Key. Copy it somewhere safe — you'll paste it into the platform at the end.
  3. Register your Brand: Messaging → 10DLC → Brands → Create Brand. Enter your legal business name, EIN / Tax ID, business address, website, and a contact. These must match your real registered business — mismatches are the #1 cause of rejection.
  4. Register a Campaign under that brand: 10DLC → Campaigns → Create Campaign. Pick the use case that fits recruiting outreach (commonly Mixed or Low Volume Mixed), provide 2–3 sample messages, describe how people opt in, and confirm you honor STOP / HELP keywords.
  5. Buy a number: Numbers → Buy Numbers → choose a local long-code number with both SMS and Voice features.
  6. Create a Messaging Profile (Messaging → Messaging Profiles), assign your number to it, and link the number to your approved campaign.
  7. Enable voice on the number so calls work too: assign it to a Voice / Call-Control connection (Voice → Programmable Voice). For best answer rates, also set your caller-ID name (CNAM) on the number.
  8. Connect it to the platform: in Setup → Integrations → Telnyx, paste your API key and your from-number (E.164, e.g. +13105551234), then Save and Test. Pick that number for each feature in Setup → Voice Drops / AI Vetting and in OS Text.
Timeline & cost. Brand approval is usually minutes to a day; campaign approval by the carriers typically takes 1–3 business days. Telnyx charges a small one-time brand fee and a low monthly campaign fee, plus per-message carrier fees — all billed by Telnyx to your account. Don't launch SMS campaigns until your campaign shows Approved.
Stay compliant — it protects your delivery. Only text people who opted in, include your business name in messages, and always honor STOP (opt-out) and HELP. Carriers police this; violations get a campaign suspended. Voice calls follow calling-hour and consent rules in your region.

Once your Telnyx key is connected and your number is on an approved campaign, all calls and SMS across Voice Drops, AI Vetting, the dialer, and OS Text run through your own number. You can swap the number a feature uses at any time without re-registering.

Branding

Make the portal unmistakably yours, in Setup → Branding:

  • Brand name: replaces the wordmark, the browser tab, and the text on your sign-in page.
  • Accent color: recolors buttons and highlights across the whole portal.
  • Logo: upload one logo for dark appearance and one for light; the sidebar swaps automatically when you toggle appearance. (Set just one and it's used for both.)
  • Fit & size: when you upload, a quick adjuster lets you drag and zoom so the logo sits perfectly, and a Logo size slider scales it up or down. Large images are welcome.

Your branding shows everywhere, sidebar, browser tab, your sign-in and signup pages, and this Help Center.

Your own domain

Run the portal on your own web address so your team and candidates only ever see your brand.

  1. Go to Setup → Custom domain and enter your domain (for example app.yourcompany.com).
  2. Add the two DNS records it shows you at your domain provider, one points traffic at the platform, one proves you own the domain.
  3. Click Verify. Once verified and live, your team signs in at your URL, fully branded.
DNS can take a few minutes to propagate. If verification doesn't pass on the first try, give it a moment and retry.

Dashboard

Your at-a-glance view of the operation: active prospects, meetings today and this week, warm conversations, replies waiting on you, and your active campaigns. A capacity strip shows how much sending headroom you have, so you know when to add more.

Candidates

Your people store for recruiting. Import lists, search and enrich contacts, and promote the best into active campaigns. Candidates and prospects are kept separate on purpose, so your sourcing pool and your active outreach never get tangled.

Campaigns & the Sequences Library

A campaign is a goal with an audience and a multi-step sequence that runs across channels, email, LinkedIn, SMS, and voice. Build and edit the steps in the Campaign Studio: choose the channel for each touch, the timing, and the message.

The Sequences Library holds reusable multi-channel templates (including LinkedIn voice notes and voicemail drops) that you can drop into any campaign and tweak. Campaigns activate once the required tools for that mode are green in Setup.

Outreach & replies

Every reply across every channel lands in one inbox. Each one is auto-classified (interested, not now, referral, stop) and routed to the right recruiter. Positive replies are surfaced first so your team books the hot ones before anything goes cold, and opt-outs are honored automatically everywhere.

JD Sourcing Recruiting

Paste a job description and the platform finds and ranks matching candidates for you. Stage the results under a name, review them, and promote the best into your Candidates store and into a campaign. A fast way to go from an open role to a worked pipeline.

Hire Signals BD

Companies that are hiring right now surface as fresh BD leads, along with the role they're hiring for, so you can reach out at exactly the right moment with a reason to talk.

OS Text Recruiting

Two-way texting for recruiting, built in. After a short go-live checklist (business registration, your number, and consent), you can text candidates and see replies right alongside your other channels. Opt-outs are honored automatically across every channel.

AI Vetting Recruiting

Screen candidates by phone, automatically. Set up a "desk" by binding a job description, your phone number, and your own cloned voice. Candidates opt in and receive a call from an AI recruiter that asks your questions in your voice. Every call is recorded, transcribed, and scored, and you review a scorecard for each one. Swap the number on a desk whenever you need to.

Voice Drops

Drop a personalized voicemail in your own cloned voice straight to a contact's landline, inside their local calling window, with consent and identification built in. A respectful way to re-engage at scale without dialing live, available in both modes.

Analytics

Outcomes over time: replies, meetings booked, and conversion broken down by campaign and by recruiter. See what's working, spot what's stalling, and double down on the plays that land.

Billing & plan

View your plan and manage billing from the account menu in the top-right, under Billing & plan. Your usage of the tools you connect is billed by those tools directly to your own accounts.
